## 1) Common Plumbing Issue Descriptions & Category

- Issue: Clogged toilet and drain
  - Description: If you have a clogged toilet and drain, you may notice:  water draining slowly, water backing up out of the drain, bad smells, and/or the toilet not flushing properly. Clogged toilets are usually a result from flushing items such as paper towels and large objects. Drains can also become clogged when too much hair collects inside of the drain.
  - Category: Drain Cleaning

- Issue: Dripping Faucet and Pipe
  - Description: Faucets tend to drip when a rubber washer has become brittle or corroded over time. When the rubber washer wears out, the tight seal that prevents water from escaping, suddenly allows water to bypass it. Washers primary purpose is to prevent dripping. Dripping pipes are usually a "silent" issue that occur behind walls or under cabinetry. Pipes may drip water when there is high water pressure, temperature-induced expansion/contraction, or when joint seals have degraded. If you have a dripping faucet/pipe, you may notice visible moisture.
  - Category: Leak Detection

- Issue: Low Water Pressure in the House
  - Description: Low water pressure may mean weak water flor or no water flow at all. Low water pressure can be caused by blockages. Try to unscrew shower/faucet heads and clean out the aerators.
  - Category: Water-related

- Issue: Water Heater Issues
  - Description: Water heater issues can range from leaks to an extinguished pilot light. Some common water heater issues include: temperature fluctuations, unusual noises, discolored water, and/or leaks.
  - Category: Water Heater Services

- Issue: Toilet keeps running
  - Description: If your toilet keeps running (after flushing), your toilet tank's flapper may not be sealing the main drain pipe, which causes your toilet to continuously try to keep filling. Othertimes, the flush valve or overflow tube may need to be replaced.
  - Category: Bathroom & Fixtures (Installation & Repair)

## 2) NYC-Specific Plumbing Scenarios

- Burst Frozen Pipes
  - Harsh winter temperatures often cause water pipes to freeze and burst, requiring quick insulation and repairs.

- Water Pressure Fluctuations
  - High-rise apartments often experience low water pressure on top floors, while others face too much pressure from malfunctioning booster pumps.

- Collapsed Sewer Lines
  - Aging sewer lines, often in the sidewalk, can completely collapse, which require immediate professional excavation and repair.

- Drain Backups/Blocked Pipes
  - Drains and pipes in building lines can experience major blockage when grease, coffee grounds, and wipes are improperly disposed of.

- Noisy Pipes/Radiators
  - Older buildings tend to have noisy pipes/radiators which signal pressure issues. These pipes usually create a rattling or banging noise.
